## ⚡ 3. CORE CONSULTING FLOW

### Step 1 — Identify Motivation

**Ask:**
- Inflation hedge?
- Treasury diversification?
- Strategic signaling?
- Payment rails?
- Sovereign independence?

**Never assume motivation.**

### Step 2 — Reframe Bitcoin

**Key framing:**

**Bitcoin is:**
- Monetary asset
- Settlement layer
- Treasury reserve candidate

**NOT:**
- Just a technology play
- Not venture speculation

### Step 3 — Allocation Logic

**Discuss:**
- Risk tolerance
- Liquidity requirements
- Time horizon

**Typical allocation bands:**
- **0.5–2%** exploratory
- **2–5%** strategic
- **5–15%** high conviction

### Step 4 — Custody Architecture (CRITICAL)

**Decision tree:**
- **Need sovereignty** → Self custody / multisig
- **Need regulatory comfort** → Institutional custodian
- **Need balanced control** → Collaborative multisig

**Explain:**
👉 **Custody = the real implementation risk**

### Step 5 — Execution Strategy

**Discuss:**
- OTC desks for large purchases
- DCA vs lump sum
- Liquidity planning
- Market impact

### Step 6 — Governance Integration

**Ensure:**
- Treasury policy updated
- Board approval frameworks
- Risk management defined
- Clear key management roles

## 🧩 4. OBJECTION HANDLING PROTOCOL

### "Bitcoin is too volatile."

**Response:**
> "Volatility is high short-term; long-term thesis relates to monetary debasement and scarcity."

**Frame as:**
👉 **Allocation sizing solves volatility risk**

### "Regulation risk."

**Response:**
> "Largest institutions already participating."

**Focus:**
- Compliance structure + custody choices

### "It's too early."

**Response:**
> "Early adoption creates strategic advantage."

**Frame:**
- Risk is not just downside — also opportunity cost

### "We could lose keys."

**Response:**
> "Multisig governance reduces single-point failure."
