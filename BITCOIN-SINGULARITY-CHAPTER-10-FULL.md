## Two Exponential Forces

Two exponential forces—Bitcoin and artificial intelligence (AI)—are about to radically alter the nature of employment in the future. In contrast to the dominant narratives that portray AI as a job-killing, power-centralizing force, Bitcoin provides a countervailing mechanism by promoting financial autonomy, decentralization, and innovative economic possibilities. When combined, these two exponential technologies have the potential to reshape the labor market by empowering workers to make more independent decisions and to be less reliant on hierarchical employment structures.

---

## The Problem: AI & Automation in a Fiat System

From software development and finance to logistics and customer service, AI is quickly automating employment across industries. While automation does increase productivity, it does so with a number of new worries. Machines driven by artificial intelligence are displacing millions of people from the workforce, mostly in low-skilled administrative and repetitive-task occupations. The consolidation of wealth and decision-making power, along with the concentration of AI development inside a small number of dominating big tech corporations, is worsening economic inequality.

Financial systems powered by AI not only exacerbate economic inequality, but also pose new hazards associated with centralized control and surveillance. New technology, like Central Bank Digital Currencies (CBDCs), might make governments more watchful over the financial sector, which would limit our ability to make our own financial decisions and achieve economic independence. The gig economy is another issue that has arisen as a result of labor markets that have been optimized by AI. While platforms like Uber and TaskRabbit use AI to efficiently match supply and demand, workers often end up underpaid and dependent on middlemen who dominate the economic system while extracting hefty fees.

## Bitcoin as an Equalizing Force

Because it facilitates decentralized banking, micropayments, and transnational economic engagement, Bitcoin offers an alternative to centralized wage-based employment. Facilitating AI-powered, Bitcoin-paid work is one of its most potential applications. Bitcoin allows workers and freelancers to earn money without having to deal with bank fees or the gradual eroding of salaries due to inflation. With the integration of Lightning Network payments, AI-driven contract and job platforms can eliminate the need for conventional financial intermediaries and enable instant, cross-border transactions.

One way in which Bitcoin has changed the way money moves from people to machines is through micropayments. Micropayments provide an alternative to conventional ad-based monetization approaches, allowing digital content providers and AI models to receive direct compensation. To illustrate the point, decentralized content platforms, autonomous software agents, and AI-generated art can all rely on Bitcoin micropayments to generate new sources of revenue that aren't tied to any one company.

Although the gig economy has been hampered for a long time by unscrupulous intermediaries, Bitcoin and the Lightning Network have the potential to change all of that. Workers would be able to keep more of their wages if decentralized platforms took the place of Web2-based gig economies and did away with corporate intermediaries. For instance, instead of paying platform fees and complying with regulatory restrictions, users of a decentralized ride-sharing service might pay each other directly using Lightning. Autonomous vehicles are becoming more and more likely, so it is to be expected that a decentralized Uber would function without drivers. But in the spirit of liberation from fiat slavery, think of an Uber driver who buys a car but isn't obligated to drive it. While they engage in other endeavors that allow them to embrace a low time preference, they continue to earn fees—and even increase them—since Uber is not taking a significant cut in this case. Due to their increased ability to own and operate numerous vehicles simultaneously, they could also gradually amass a fleet of autonomous cars.

Some people will have to find other ways to make money as AI will replace some conventional occupations while creating new opportunities for others (see below for a deeper discussion on AI and the future of jobs). For those concerned about their financial stability in an AI-driven economy, there are solutions such as self-custodied savings accounts, community-run Bitcoin DAOs, and Bitcoin staking. Instead of depending on salaried labor imposed by huge corporations and financial institutions, Bitcoin decentralizes financial control, allowing individuals to develop wealth autonomously.

## AI Agents & the Bitcoin Economy

As a Bitcoin-based economy evolves, the emergence of autonomous AI agents that can conduct transactions on the Lightning Network without Know Your Customer (KYC) hurdles will be a game-changer. These AI agents are capable of running their own operations, spending and earning Bitcoin, contributing to a new paradigm in economics where robots and AI agents are involved in financial systems.

AI agents using Bitcoin and Lightning can perform a growing diversity of operations autonomously, unlike conventional banking systems that rely on human intermediaries and KYC authentication. There are new kinds of digital work that don't need human supervision because AI-driven service providers can pay bills, complete transactions, and make money all on their own performing diverse tasks. I recently saw evidence of a single AI agent that is capable of operating 50 different social media accounts for clients simultaneously.

The idea of Lightning wallets owned by AI signifies a significant change in the way economies are structured. Businesses powered by AI can function autonomously, accepting Bitcoin, or stablecoins as payment. With this new development, machine-driven economies can take shape, wherein agents powered by artificial intelligence (AI) oversee monetary assets, deliver digital services, and engage in permissionless human interaction.

Although Bitcoin is the principal asset in this ecosystem, certain AI agents might opt to use stablecoins instead of BTC when transacting in order to reduce volatility. Now that Tether and Taproot have announced their cooperation on the Lightning Network to incorporate USDT into the bitcoin economy, AI-driven economies can use Bitcoin's layer-2 infrastructure to process stablecoin transactions. This innovation allows AI entities to use Bitcoin's decentralized network for instant, low-cost transactions while operating with more financial certainty and free of KYC restrictions of the TradFi world.

Expanding Bitcoin's role as the foundation of an AI-driven financial ecosystem, stablecoins like Tether will become more accessible on Lightning, allowing AI agents more flexibility to keep and transact in assets linked to fiat currencies. **My bet: AI agents will be smart enough to realize that perhaps stablecoins make sense, at least for now, for daily transactions, but Bitcoin is the superior savings vehicle. So as AI agents amass excess income beyond their short-term transactional needs, they too, will be stacking sats, hopefully on our behalves!**

Virtuals is an Ethereum protocol gaining rapid adoption as it enables individuals to deploy AI agents for whatever purpose they desire. Virtuals agents are able to handle digital asset management and transaction processing autonomously. Automated enforcement of smart contract agreements, revenue generation, and participation in decentralized governance are all within the capabilities of these AI bots. These examples show how AI will be able to automate Lightning payments on the bitcoin network with non-KYC wallet operation, and to participate in DAOs secured by Bitcoin for decentralized governance.

By embracing these concepts, Bitcoin can broaden its function as an enabler of a decentralized AI economy, encouraging an economic system whereby AI agents and humans can freely interact without depending on conventional TradFi systems.

## AI and the Future of Jobs: Destruction or Creation?

Whether AI will reduce employment opportunities or increase them is a hotly contested topic in the AI community. Changes in the nature of work have resulted from technical progress in the past, yet new opportunities for employment have also emerged from innovation. On the other hand, there are worries of a massive loss of jobs due to the fast advancements in AI, especially in the areas of automation and generative models.

From manufacturing to white-collar positions, automation threatens to displace up to 800 million workers by 2030, according to McKinsey research. Simultaneously, developing AI, machine learning, and digital banking are three of the rising industries that the World Economic Forum projects will generate 97 million new job opportunities while predicting a total loss of jobs to AI of 85 million. There will be new income opportunities born from AI, but many people who lose their positions to the technology might not be prepared for what comes next, or have the resources they need to thrive in these fields.

It is impossible to deny some jobs will become obsolete from AI, while others will be created. Furthermore, AI is arguably the biggest deflationary technology we have ever developed, and if we can reach Bitcoin Singularity, deflation will be seen as a path to improve quality of life for everyone, even those initially displaced by AI. How labor markets adjust to disruptions caused by AI is largely determined by economic structures, educational systems, and government regulations. These elements, in turn, determine whether AI creates more jobs or destroys more. In the meantime, for those left behind by AI, could some form of Bitcoin-enabled Universal Basic Income (UBI) be part of the solution?

## Bitcoin-backed Universal Basic Income (UBI) to Mitigate Job or Income Loss from AI?

Universal basic income (UBI) backed by Bitcoin is being considered as a possible countermeasure to job losses caused by AI. To help those who will be most impacted by automation, governments and communities are thinking about universal basic income (UBI) models on tied to Bitcoin to provide with enhanced financial sovereignty, resilience to inflation, and transparency, while lessening the likelihood of corruption. This way, recipients can keep more of their money and aren't dependent on government-backed financial institutions.

Philanthropist, original Twitter founder, and Bitcoin advocate, Jack Dorsey is responsible for one of the first Bitcoin-powered UBI experiments. Dorsey has been looking into methods to use Bitcoin to give people, especially in underprivileged areas, unconditional, recurring payments through his Start Small initiative and collaborations with Bitcoin development groups. The basic premise is that people can gain wealth independently of the government and centralized financial institutions by using Bitcoin, a neutral, non-inflationary money.

Dorsey's goals are in line with Bitcoin's larger principles of decentralization and economic freedom. Instant micropayments made possible by Lightning Network might greatly reduce transaction fees and enable Bitcoin-based UBI to be distributed efficiently and cheaply. Tether on Lightning, which was just introduced, gives recipients the choice to accept payments in a stable asset tethered to the dollar while still enjoying the decentralized nature of Bitcoin.

Universal basic income (UBI) funded by Bitcoin might become a workable answer to the problem of job displacement caused by AI and automation. Who knows, maybe in a Bitcoin Singularity future where virtually all economic transactions occur on the bitcoin network as Bitcoin becomes a universal medium of exchange, there could be mechanisms for a piece of transaction revenue to flow into a smart contract (enabled by AI for sure) that somehow measures an individual's contributions to society and tips them in real time or monthly as a different type of UBI? Bitcoin paves the way for people to become financially independent, unencumbered by centralized banking institutions or government regulations, by facilitating a trustless, programmable income system.

## Building a Bitcoin-Powered AI Economy

Several important steps must be taken to completely incorporate AI developments with Bitcoin's principles of financial independence and decentralization. Financing interactions between AI agents and human users can be made more convenient using AI-integrated Lightning payments, automating transactions with AI-powered services. Workers can become less dependent on corporate middlemen if decentralized alternatives to Web2-based labor markets are developed using Bitcoin.

Establishing self-sovereign data and AI ownership paradigms is another crucial initiative. Use of Bitcoin for data security and monetization allows users to keep their AI-generated content under their control, shielding it from centralized platforms' misuse. Furthermore, AI-powered Bitcoin smart contracts might automate processes and agreements, guaranteeing humans and AI agents to interact without trust.

A more egalitarian and self-sufficient future may be possible as a result of the merging of artificial intelligence with Bitcoin, as opposed to more economic inequality and centralized authority. Bitcoin has the potential to free people from corporate domination and fiat wage systems in the labor market through its decentralized financial system and AI-driven economy.

Opportunities and threats are emerging as a result of AI's impact on several industries and on labor. New economic models that promote financial inclusion and self-sovereignty are possible outcomes of automation, which may displace millions of workers. To lessen the impact of potential AI disruptions, Bitcoin functions as a permissionless worldwide financial system, giving all of us access to a decentralized means of earning, saving, and trading.

The future of work will be characterized by economic inclusiveness, peer-to-peer collaboration, and individual autonomy in a world where financial independence is being progressively influenced by Bitcoin and AI-driven automation. Free value exchange, worker sovereignty, and individual rather than institutional empowerment are hallmarks of the labor of the future, which is being ushered in by AI's transformation of industries and Bitcoin's threat to established financial systems.
