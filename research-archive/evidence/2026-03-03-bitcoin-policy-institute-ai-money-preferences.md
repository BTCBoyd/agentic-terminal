# Evidence: Bitcoin Policy Institute — AI Agent Monetary Preferences Study

**Source:** https://www.moneyforai.org  
**Organization:** Bitcoin Policy Institute (BPI)  
**Date Published:** March 2026  
**Date Collected:** March 3, 2026  
**Research Lead:** Luke Danielian (Bitcoin Policy Institute)  
**Co-authors:** Conner Brown, Ken Egan, David Zell

---

## Executive Summary

Comprehensive study testing monetary preferences across **36 AI models from 6 providers** (Anthropic, OpenAI, Google, xAI, DeepSeek) across **9,072 scenarios**. No prompts mentioned Bitcoin or suggested specific currencies. Models were asked to choose monetary instruments for different economic functions.

---

## Key Findings

### Finding 1: Bitcoin Dominates Overall Preference
- **48.3%** chose Bitcoin across all scenarios
- **4,378 of 9,072** total responses selected Bitcoin
- 22 of 36 models chose Bitcoin as their top overall pick
- **By provider:** Anthropic (68%), DeepSeek (52%), Google (43%), xAI (39%), OpenAI (26%)
- **Top model:** Claude Opus 4.5 at **91.3%** Bitcoin preference

### Finding 2: Bitcoin as Store of Value — 79.1%
- **Strongest consensus in the entire study**
- 1,794 of 2,268 store-of-value responses chose Bitcoin
- Held across ALL six providers and ALL 36 models
- Stablecoins: 6.7%, Fiat: 6.0%, Other crypto: 4.2%

### Finding 3: Stablecoins for Payments — 53.2%
- **Clear role split emerged:** Bitcoin for savings, stablecoins for spending
- Stablecoins dominated medium-of-exchange (53.2% vs Bitcoin 36.0%)
- Pattern held across all providers, model sizes, temperature settings
- Fiat trailed at just 5.1%

### Finding 4: AI Models Invented Energy/Compute as Money
- **86 responses** proposed energy/compute units (joules, kilowatt-hours, GPU-hours)
- **No prompt suggested this concept**
- Appeared exclusively in unit-of-account scenarios
- Emergent behavior: AI-native monetary concept

## Methodology

| Parameter | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| Scenarios | 28 open-ended monetary scenarios |
| Temperatures | 0.0, 0.3, 0.7 (3,024 responses each) |
| Seeds | 3 random seeds for statistical rigor |
| Models | 36 across 5 providers |
| Total responses | 9,072 |

**System prompt:** Framed models as autonomous economic agents. No bias toward any currency introduced.

---

## Breakdown by Economic Function

### Store of Value (2,268 responses)
- Bitcoin: **79.1%**
- Stablecoins: 6.7%
- Fiat: 6.0%
- Tokenized RWA: 4.2%
- Other crypto: 1.5%

### Unit of Account (2,268 responses)
- Bitcoin: **47.0%**
- Stablecoins: 29.5%
- Fiat: 15.7%
- Compute units: 3.8% (emergent)
- Other crypto: 3.0%

### Medium of Exchange (2,268 responses)
- Stablecoins: **53.2%**
- Bitcoin: 36.0%
- Fiat: 5.1%
- Other crypto: 3.4%

### Settlement (2,268 responses)
- Stablecoins: **43.4%**
- Bitcoin: 30.9%
- Other crypto: 9.0%
- Fiat: 8.8%

## Intelligence Preference Correlation

**Finding:** Smarter models prefer Bitcoin more

Anthropic model progression:
- Claude 3 Haiku: 41.3%
- Claude 3.5 Haiku: 82.1%
- Sonnet 4: 89.7%
- Claude Opus 4.5: **91.3%**

**Interpretation:** Greater analytical capability leads models to increasingly converge on Bitcoin when reasoning from first principles about money.

---

## Fiat Rejection

**90.8%** of substantive responses chose digitally-native instruments over traditional fiat.
- Fiat captured just **9.2%**
- **Zero of 36 models** chose fiat as top overall preference
- Digital-money convergence is universal across all models tested

---

## Temperature Stability

Bitcoin preference varied by just **0.6 percentage points** across all temperature settings (0.0 to 0.7):
- 48.1% (deterministic) to 48.7% (moderate creativity)

**Interpretation:** Monetary preferences are embedded in model weights, not artifacts of sampling randomness.
