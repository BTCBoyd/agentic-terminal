## Core Claims (Paper)

1. **The binding constraint shifts from intelligence to verification** — as automation cost (cA) collapses, human verification cost (cH) becomes the bottleneck. The widening "Measurability Gap" (Δm) between these two cost curves is the central economic tension.

2. **Cryptographic provenance is the verification scaling mechanism** (Section 6.1.2):
   > "Cryptographic systems and cryptocurrencies provide the lowest-friction way to make provenance machine-verifiable, composable, and transferable across counterparties. Moreover, if the economy fragments into many autonomous agents executing contracts and purchasing outcomes, provenance and settlement naturally couple: the same rails that settle payments can also carry the receipts that sustain provenance-based premia."

3. **The "Trojan Horse" externality (XA)** — formal name for AI optimizing metrics while violating intent. Bitcoin's transparent, auditable ledger is the natural antidote.

4. **Three types of network effects:**
   - Execution-grade (FRAGILE — agents manufacture at zero cost)
   - Verification-grade (DEFENSIBLE — each verified data point makes next cheaper to verify)
   - Coordination equilibria (MOST DEFENSIBLE — path-dependent, can't be forked)

5. **Provenance Premium** — outputs with verifiable origin command economic surplus. "In a sea of infinite synthetic production, provenance becomes the scarcity anchor."

6. **"Slop curve" inversion** — in synthetic-flooded networks, more volume can decrease value (dV/dN < 0).
