### 4. Abundance Capitalism (Unpublished Manuscript)
**File:** `abundance-capitalism.md`  
**Author:** Boyd Cohen (pre-Bitcoin Singularity)  
**Status:** Never publicly released (used for MBA teaching)  
**Coverage:**
- **5 Principles of Abundance Capitalism**: Systems thinking, multi-stakeholder collaboration, current viability, economic necessity, planetary boundaries
- **Planetary boundaries**: 6 of 9 crossed as of 2023 (Stockholm Resilience Institute)
- **Circular economy**: Cradle-to-cradle, biomimicry, regenerative business models
- **Climate moonshots**: Fusion energy, SAF 2.0 (carbon-to-fuel), BESS, green steel/cement
- **Regenerative agriculture**: Indigo Ag case study, carbon sequestration at scale
- **15-minute cities**: Paris, Melbourne, Portland models
- **Fab cities**: Local production (70% by 2054 goal), digital fabrication
- **Copenhagen model**: Waste-to-energy + carbon capture + ski slope
- **M-Pesa case study**: Financial inclusion through mobile banking + blockchain
- **Healthcare transformation**: AI + biotechnology for proactive, predictive medicine
- **Education reform**: 7 pillars including interdisciplinary learning, Singularity University model
- **Expert interviews**: Hunter Lovins, Catherine Weetman, Professor Desirée Pacheco

**Foundational Thesis:**
> "Abundance Capitalism: An innovative economic model that combines the growth spirit of capitalism with deep commitment to sustainability and abundance. Challenges traditional scarcity mindset by imagining a world where essential resources are universally accessible, sustained by ecological balance."

**Connection to Sustainable Abundance Triad:**
This book provides the sustainability/abundance framework that Bitcoin Singularity later built upon. It's the "why abundance is possible" foundation before Bitcoin became the "how to achieve it" mechanism.

**Use Cases:**
- Understanding Boyd's pre-Bitcoin sustainability worldview
- Context for Sustainable Abundance Triad (Bitcoin + AI + Longevity)
- Regenerative business model education
- Systems thinking applied to climate/energy/food/water
- Circular economy vs. linear economy arguments
- Corporate sustainability strategy (Interface, Danone, Coca-Cola case studies)
