# CONTENT-FORMATTING-GUIDELINES.md
## Human-Readable Content Standards (Boyd's Rules)

**MANDATORY: Read this before publishing ANY Substack article, newsletter, or long-form content.**

---

## 1. Paragraph Spacing — LESS IS MORE

- **Minimal blank lines between paragraphs.** Do NOT add an extra blank line after every paragraph like a markdown document.
- Substack and newsletter formats should flow like a magazine article, not a GitHub README.
- One line break between paragraphs is enough. Double-spacing makes posts feel empty and hard to read.

## 2. Bold & Italics — Use Them with Purpose

- **Bold** key terms, company names, and the main concept at the start of a list item.
  - Example: In a list of 5 criteria, bold the thing being described: **ACINQ** — top 5 globally...
  - Example: New section label in a paragraph: **The Two-Channel Strategy** — here's why...
- *Italics* for emphasis on a specific word, quotes, or a secondary point worth highlighting.
- These break up monotony and give the reader's eye somewhere to land. Use them intentionally, not decoratively.
- **Never use bold/italic for entire sentences or paragraphs** — only for the key word/phrase within them.

## 3. Every Article Needs at Least One Graphic

- **No exceptions.** Every Substack post, newsletter, or long-form article must include at least one visual.
- Best options (in order of preference):
  1. Comparison table (like the wallet/channel partner dark-theme tables)
  2. Chart or data visualization
  3. Branded header image with title
  4. Screenshot of relevant data
- The graphic should be the HEADER image of the post AND can be reused inline.
- **Table format:** Use the dark-theme AT style (dark brown background, orange headers, ✅ ❌ 🟡 icons, ⭐ star ratings). See: `/research-archive/articles/channel-partner-table.html`

## 4. Article Structure That Reads Well

- Short intro (2-3 sentences max) that hooks immediately — no throat-clearing
- Section headers to break the article into scannable chunks
- Lists of 3-7 items with **bolded lead word** + explanatory sentence
- Closing paragraph that lands the thesis cleanly
- Byline at the end pointing to agenticterminal.substack.com

## 5. Substack-Specific Notes

- Substack does NOT have a native table editor — use images for comparison tables
- Subscribe buttons should be added (Substack prompts this on publish)
- Thumbnail/header image = same as the main graphic (saves design time)
