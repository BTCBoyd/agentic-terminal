## Lessons Learned

**Research Approach:**
- Initial strategy assumed standard online cohort model
- Boyd's update (12-session Executive MBA) required significant recalibration
- Key insight: Positioning matters more than platform (Executive MBA vs online course = 2-3x pricing power)
- Bilingual delivery = underserved market opportunity (Spanish-language Bitcoin education nearly non-existent at executive level)

**Platform Research:**
- Web search API unavailable (Brave API key missing), used direct web_fetch instead
- Canvas, Maven, Teachable, Podia, Kajabi all successfully researched
- MIT, NYU Stern, IESE pricing benchmarks validated Executive MBA positioning

**Competitive Analysis:**
- Confirmed zero direct competitors teaching Bitcoin×AI convergence at Executive MBA level
- Saylor/Hope.com = free (creates awareness, not competition)
- Lyn Alden = research subscription ($200-500/year), not structured course
- Andreas Antonopoulos = technical workshops, not executive
- Gap validated: No Spanish-language Executive Bitcoin education exists

**Key Insight:**
- Boyd's competitive moat is not just "Bitcoin course" but:
  1. PhD credibility + Executive MBA pedagogy
  2. Bilingual (English/Spanish) delivery
  3. Lightning Network hands-on immersion
  4. Government/corporate consulting credentials
  5. 12-session depth (not typical 4-8 week course)
- This moat justifies $7K-12K pricing (vs typical $2K-4K online courses)

**Economic Reality Check:**
- Year 1 revenue target: $350K-500K (realistic based on 4 cohorts + 1 corporate contract)
- Corporate/government contracts = massive LTV multiplier (1 contract = $75K-150K vs 15 individual students = $105K)
- Priority: Land 1-2 corporate/gov contracts in Year 1 to validate enterprise positioning

**What Would I Do Differently:**
- Ask for Boyd's existing course outline earlier (would have saved time on generic 8-week structure)
- Focus more on Executive MBA benchmarks upfront (MIT, NYU, IESE pricing research should have been Day 1)
- Bilingual opportunity should have been highlighted earlier (Spanish-language Bitcoin education = blue ocean)
