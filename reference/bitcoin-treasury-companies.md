# Bitcoin Treasury Companies: Overview, Key Players, and Emerging Models

## 1. What is a Bitcoin Treasury Company?

A Bitcoin treasury company is an organization that treats Bitcoin as a primary strategic balance sheet asset rather than merely an investment or speculative holding.

At the most basic level, this involves replacing some or all corporate treasury reserves (cash, bonds, etc.) with Bitcoin.

However, modern treasury companies go further:

- Use capital markets to acquire additional Bitcoin.
- Engineer equity structures tied to BTC performance.
- Transform Bitcoin into a financial product layer.

There are two broad categories:

### A. Bitcoin Treasury Strategy (BTS)

- Company holds Bitcoin as reserve asset.
- Passive accumulation.
- Example: Tesla (historically), Block.

### B. Leveraged Bitcoin Equity (LBE)

Defined in Swan's framework as companies that:

- Actively raise capital (equity, convertibles, debt) to acquire BTC.
- Seek increasing Bitcoin-per-share over time.
- Treat equity itself as a structured Bitcoin exposure product.

(See Swan LBE framework for definitions and mechanics.)

## 2. Core Strategic Thesis

### The Saylor Thesis: Digital Capital → Digital Credit

Michael Saylor reframed Bitcoin treasury strategy as:

1. Bitcoin = superior long-duration capital asset ("digital capital").
2. Equity markets price companies with BTC exposure at a premium.
3. That premium can be used to raise capital.
4. Raised capital is converted into more Bitcoin.
5. Result: recursive accumulation.

Key concepts:

- Bitcoin per share as primary KPI.
- mNAV (market value vs net asset value multiple).
- Equity becomes a financial instrument representing leveraged BTC exposure.

This transforms the company from operating business → capital engine.

## 3. Evolution of the Model

### Phase 1 — Bitcoin on Balance Sheet

Companies added BTC as hedge against fiat debasement.

Examples:

- Tesla
- Block
- Coinbase (partial)

Characteristics:

- Limited leverage.
- Bitcoin is treasury diversification.

---

### Phase 2 — Leveraged Bitcoin Equity (LBE)

Pioneered by Strategy (the company formerly known as MicroStrategy, rebranded 2024).

Characteristics:

- Bitcoin becomes dominant balance sheet asset.
- Capital markets used aggressively:
  - Convertible debt
  - ATM equity issuance
  - Structured instruments

Goal:

Increase BTC per share continuously.

According to Swan research, LBE companies aim to engineer volatility and treat equity as a product.

---

### Phase 3 — Bitcoin Treasury 2.0 (Emerging)

This is the evolution represented by newer players and thinkers like Jack Mallers and emerging models like XXI.

Key shift:

NOT just treasury accumulation.

Instead:

- Integrated Bitcoin-native services + treasury.
- Treasury becomes strategic core enabling ecosystem growth.

Characteristics:

- Bitcoin treasury + financial services stack.
- Synergistic products:
  - payments
  - lending
  - custody
  - Lightning infrastructure
  - advisory

This aligns with ArcadiaB-style thinking:

> Treasury is not the business — treasury is the engine powering the business ecosystem.

## 6. Core Metrics

Key metrics emphasized in LBE analysis:

- Bitcoin per share.
- Bitcoin yield (growth rate of BTC/share).
- mNAV (market value vs BTC net asset value).
- Leverage ratio.
- Capital market execution efficiency.

(These are highlighted heavily in Swan's LBE framework.)

## 7. Critiques and Risks

A balanced perspective includes:

### Financial Risks

- Leverage amplifies downside.
- Dependence on equity premium.
- Debt maturity timing risk.
- Interest rate exposure.

### Structural Concerns

- Companies may become pseudo-ETFs.
- Operational business relevance may decline.
- Premium-to-NAV may compress.

### Market Cycle Risk

If BTC stagnates:

- Treasury accumulation thesis weakens.
- Capital market access may slow.
