## RISK FACTORS & MITIGATION

### Market Risks

**1. Bitcoin Bear Market**
- **Risk**: If BTC crashes 50-70%, interest in Bitcoin education may wane
- **Mitigation**: Emphasize AI component (still relevant); position as "buy when there's blood in the streets" education
- **Historical Note**: Lyn Alden grew subscribers during 2022 bear market (people seek education in downturns)

**2. AI Hype Fatigue**
- **Risk**: "AI fatigue" if overhyped in 2026-2027
- **Mitigation**: Focus on practical business application, not hype; differentiate with Bitcoin convergence angle

**3. Regulatory Crackdown**
- **Risk**: Governments crack down on Bitcoin (unlikely but possible)
- **Mitigation**: Position as educational (not investment advice); diversify content to include "what if Bitcoin fails?" scenarios

---

### Execution Risks

**1. Boyd's Time Availability**
- **Risk**: Running live cohorts is time-intensive; may burn out
- **Mitigation**: Hire VA/course assistant ($1K-2K/cohort); transition to Teachable after Cohort 3-5 for passive income

**2. Content Creation Overwhelm**
- **Risk**: Creating 8 weeks of high-quality content is a lot of work
- **Mitigation**: Start with 70% curriculum ready (co-create with Cohort 1); record live sessions for reuse

**3. Low Enrollment (Founding Cohort)**
- **Risk**: What if only 3-5 people sign up?
- **Mitigation**: Run cohort with minimum 8 students (still profitable at 8 × $3,497 = $27,976); if <8, offer 1-on-1 consulting version at $10K

---

### Competitive Risks

**1. Copycats**
- **Risk**: Someone copies Boyd's Bitcoin×AI thesis after course launches
- **Mitigation**: First-mover advantage + PhD credibility + brand = defensible moat; continuously update curriculum

**2. Incumbent Platforms Enter Space**
- **Risk**: Coursera, Udemy, or LinkedIn Learning launch Bitcoin×AI courses
- **Mitigation**: Boyd's cohort model + premium positioning + niche audience = different value prop (community vs. content)
