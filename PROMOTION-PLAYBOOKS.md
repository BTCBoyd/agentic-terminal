# How to Promote Without Being Promotional
## Playbooks for Value-First Strategic Content

**Purpose:** Teach subagents HOW to promote launches, events, and products without sounding like marketing.

---

## The Core Principle

**Lead with value. Let the promotion be the supporting detail.**

Our audience (UHNWI, family offices, Bitcoin thought leaders) is sophisticated. They recognize marketing immediately and filter it out. The only promotion that works for institutional audiences is content that would be valuable even WITHOUT the promotional element.

---

## Playbook: Launching a New Platform/Site

### ❌ BAD
"We're excited to announce capitalduro.mx! Mexico's first institutional Bitcoin research platform. Check it out!"

**Why it fails:**
- Generic announcement language ("excited to announce")
- No specific value proposition
- Asks audience to "check it out" (work for them, no clear payoff)
- Sounds like every other product launch

### ✅ GOOD
"Mexico's M2 money supply expanded 12.3% YoY. What does that mean for corporate treasurers holding peso reserves? We broke down the numbers in our latest analysis on Capital Duro → [link]"

**Why it works:**
- Leads with a specific, verifiable fact (M2 expansion)
- Poses a question the audience cares about (implications for their money)
- Promises a specific deliverable (analysis with numbers)
- Platform is the source, not the subject

### Pattern
1. Surface a specific, valuable finding from the platform
2. Frame it as solving a problem the audience has
3. Link to the platform as the source
4. Repeat across launch week with DIFFERENT findings each time

### More Examples
- "We compared Bitcoin LTV models across 5 jurisdictions. Mexico's ASOFOM structure enables something unique. Full breakdown: [Capital Duro link]"
- "60/40 portfolios barely kept pace with inflation in Mexico (2020-2025). Here's what family offices who outperformed did differently: [Capital Duro link]"

## Playbook: Promoting an Event/Speaking Engagement

### ❌ BAD
"Our CEO José Carlos is speaking at [event] in NYC! Come see him! 🎉"

**Why it fails:**
- Celebration language ("Come see him!")
- No value proposition for the audience
- Assumes they care about OUR CEO (they don't, yet)
- No hook or reason to pay attention

### ✅ GOOD
"A question we hear constantly from US institutions: how does Bitcoin lending actually work in a regulated Mexican framework? José Carlos will be unpacking this at [event] on Feb 20. The ASOFOM structure is genuinely unique and most people outside Mexico don't know it exists."

**Why it works:**
- Starts with THE QUESTION the audience actually has
- Positions the event as where the answer gets explored
- Frames Mexican framework as unique knowledge (not common)
- CEO is the messenger, not the message

### Pattern
1. Start with the QUESTION the audience cares about
2. Position the event as where the answer gets explored
3. Mention the speaker as the expert delivering the insight
4. Add one detail that creates curiosity ("most don't know this exists")

### More Examples (for different event types)
- "What happens when a Mexican family office wants Bitcoin exposure but can't sell real estate? José Carlos breaks down hybrid collateral models at [event]. These structures didn't exist 2 years ago."
- "The gap between US and Mexican Bitcoin regulatory frameworks creates arbitrage opportunities institutions are missing. [Speaker] unpacking this at [event]."

## Playbook: Promoting a Report/Research

### ❌ BAD
"New report alert! Download our Bitcoin vs Real Estate analysis."

**Why it fails:**
- Generic announcement ("report alert!")
- No specific value proposition
- Tells audience to "download" (work for them, unclear payoff)
- Could apply to any report

### ✅ GOOD
"We compared Bitcoin and AAA commercial real estate in Mexico across 12 metrics over 10 years. The results surprised us on three dimensions — portability wasn't even the biggest gap. Full analysis: [link]"

**Why it works:**
- Specific methodology (12 metrics, 10 years, Mexico)
- Teases a surprising finding (portability wasn't #1)
- Creates curiosity (what WAS the biggest gap?)
- Positions the report as the answer

### Pattern
1. State the specific comparison/analysis done
2. Share ONE genuinely surprising or counterintuitive finding
3. Create curiosity gap (tease but don't fully reveal)
4. Link to full analysis

### More Examples
- "We modeled Bitcoin-backed lending scenarios for family offices under 3 LTV ratios. The 'safe' conventional wisdom (30% LTV) actually leaves money on the table in 68% of historical scenarios. Why: [link]"
- "Real estate appreciation in CDMX premium zones (2015-2025): +110%. Bitcoin: +6,200%. But here's why the naive comparison misses the point: [link]"

## Playbook: Promoting a Partnership or Sponsorship

### ❌ BAD
"Proud to announce ArcadiaB is Platinum Sponsor of MITA TechTalks 2026!"

**Why it fails:**
- Corporate announcement language ("Proud to announce")
- Focuses on OUR involvement, not the value of the event
- No reason for audience to care
- Sounds like a press release

### ✅ GOOD
"125 executives, family office principals, and tech leaders in Punta Mita this May exploring Bitcoin, AI, and energy. This is the kind of intimate, high-signal gathering where real institutional adoption conversations happen. ArcadiaB is proud to be part of it as Platinum Sponsor."

**Why it works:**
- Describes the VALUE of the event first (who attends, what gets discussed)
- Positions it as "high-signal" (quality > quantity)
- Explains WHY it matters (real adoption conversations)
- Our sponsorship is the supporting detail, not the lead

### Pattern
1. Describe the VALUE of the event/partnership first
2. Frame it in terms the audience cares about (intimate, high-signal, decision-makers)
3. Explain why THIS event matters vs. generic conferences
4. Our involvement is mentioned last as supporting context

### More Examples
- "MITA TechTalks brings together the family office principals and institutional decision-makers actually allocating to Bitcoin - not consultants theorizing about it. This is where real treasury strategy conversations happen. ArcadiaB is sponsoring because these are OUR clients and peers."
- "Most Bitcoin conferences are retail-focused. [Event] is different - CFO-level discussions on custody, compliance, and capital efficiency. ArcadiaB is participating as [role]."

## Playbook: Amplifying a Guest Column (Perspectivas Invitadas)

### ❌ BAD
"Check out our new guest column on Capital Duro!"

**Why it fails:**
- Generic call to action ("check out")
- No specific value proposition
- Focuses on the PLATFORM, not the content
- Gives audience no reason to care

### ✅ GOOD
"A Mexican family office is using commercial real estate revenue to systematically accumulate Bitcoin. They wrote about their thesis and why they think this model scales. This is the kind of innovation happening in Mexico that nobody outside the country is talking about → [link]"

**Why it works:**
- Leads with WHAT the guest is actually doing (not just "wrote an article")
- Explains the specific thesis/approach
- Positions it as unique knowledge (happening in Mexico, not widely known)
- Capital Duro is the platform where you can read it, not the subject

### Pattern
1. Lead with what the GUEST is doing/saying that's interesting
2. Explain their thesis or approach in one sentence
3. Frame it as unique/under-discussed knowledge
4. Capital Duro is where you can read the full thing

### More Examples
- "A corporate treasurer in Monterrey is running Bitcoin lending parallel to traditional credit lines. The mechanics are fascinating - collateral models that wouldn't work in US regulatory environment. Guest column on Capital Duro: [link]"
- "One of AsoBitcoinMX's founding members breaks down how Mexican families are thinking about multi-generational Bitcoin inheritance. The legal structures are different than US/Europe. Perspectivas Invitadas: [link]"

## The Ratio Rule

**For every 1 explicitly promotional post, ensure there are at least 4 posts that week on the same platform that are purely valuable with no promotional intent.**

### Why This Matters
- Protects credibility (audience trusts accounts that educate > accounts that sell)
- Ensures variety (promotional posts are contextualized by value content)
- Sustainable (can promote indefinitely without burning out audience)

### How to Track
At end of each week, count:
- Promotional posts (launches, events, products, sponsorships)
- Value posts (education, analysis, engagement, market commentary)
- Ratio should be 1:4 or better (1:5, 1:6 is even better)

If ratio slips toward 1:3 or worse → increase value content, don't decrease promotion.

---

## When to Violate These Rules

**Almost never.** But IF there's a major time-sensitive announcement that MUST go out immediately (regulatory development, urgent company news, crisis communication), then:

1. Flag to Boyd first if possible
2. Lead with the NEWS, not the announcement language
3. Keep it factual and brief
4. Return to value-first immediately after

---

## Quality Checklist for Promotional Content

Before posting ANY promotional content, verify:

- [ ] Does this lead with VALUE the audience cares about?
- [ ] Is the promotion the SUPPORTING detail, not the subject?
- [ ] Would this post be useful even if our brand/product wasn't mentioned?
- [ ] Have I followed the specific playbook pattern for this type of promotion?
- [ ] Is this the ONLY promotional post today on this platform?
- [ ] Am I on track for 1:4 promotional-to-value ratio this week?

If ANY checkbox fails → revise or kill the post.

---

*Remember: Our audience filters out 95% of marketing. The 5% that works is content so valuable they don't realize it's promotional.*
